Senda App: Engaging Baby Boomers in the Digital Age

Strategy, Lean UX, Prototype, Branding

Project Timeline

4 months (April - August 2019)

The Team

6 people: Researcher, UX Designer, UI Designer, Visual Designe, Strategic Designer

Main Goal

Identify new opportunities that make the baby boomer generation happier in the digitale age.

The problem

There is a clear disconnection between baby boomers and digital initiatives. Boomers feel that there is still a misconception of their aspirations and they feel entirely overlooked.

The Opportunity

Digital products  - and most of the consumer goods -  currently fall into the trap of appealing only to millennials. Baby boomers want to be empowered, not patronize. They certainly are looking to find ways to stay active, engaged, relevant to society, and healthy.

They are looking for new social opportunities: reconnecting with family and friends, meeting new people who share their interests or participating in organizations that support causes they care about.

The Solution

Senda is a digital platform that lets seniors enjoy life by connecting and sharing. The platform not only features personalized audiovisual (AV) content but also support for users to create their own content. Senda provides the user assistance on becoming a Vlogger and guides them on the path that will let them play an active part in today's Digital Society.

Chrono24 Virtual Showroom App

UX Writing, Localization, Marketing, Augmented Reality

What is Chrono24 Virtual Showroom?

The Virtual Showroom from Chrono24 is a new and revolutionary watch-shopping experience.

Chrono24, the world’s biggest specialist secondary market platform for luxury watches, has developed a feature that lets the users try on watches using virtual reality technology on their smartphones.

How does it work?

Download the Chrono24 app

Download the PDF file with the instructions.

Cut out the bracelet below along the dotted line.

Put the bracelet on your wrist.

Open the Virtual Showroom in the Chrono24 app and select a model.

Move the camera to see the watch from different angles including the band and clasp.

You can see watches on your wrist or on a flat surface where they can be flipped over to look at the back.

Goal

Localizing the User Experience: Adapting the app to specific markets, taking into account cultural and linguistic differences in order to provide the best possible experience and boost convertion rates.

Project Timeline

10 months (February - November 2018)

The Team

Eight in-house trans creators and ten freelance translators (French, English, Spanish, Italian, Dutch, Russian, Japanese, Turkish). As well as the Product team, Development, the UX team and Content Services.

My Role

As the localization team coordinator, I kept the eight international team members organized and focused on the project and goals at hand.

  • Took part early in the project to ensure the cultural context Communicated with the product manager to align with the project requirements and the quality standards
  • Identified and fixed linguistic and cultural issues before launch
  • Thoroughly QA all localized content to ensure consistent messaging and proper functionality
  • Coordinated creation and maintenance of linguistic assets
  • Close collaboration with the UX writers
